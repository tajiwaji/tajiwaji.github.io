Sables are small carnivores belonging to the weasel family, Mustelidae. They range from about 13 to 20 inches long,

excluding their 5-7 inch tail, weighing 2-4 pounds. Their body color can vary from brown to almost black, sometimes

with a throat patch from dusky to salmon.

The sable has been categorized as a species of least concern by The International Union for Conservation of Nature,

with their being a population of over two million in Russia alone. They can also be found in northern Mongolia, China,

and Japan.

Sables play an important role in the health of their ecosystems by helping to control small rodents, squirrels, and

other animals. They also contribute to seed dispersal, eating nuts and berries when primary food sources are scarce.
